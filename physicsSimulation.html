About the Simulation

Purpose

This simulation aims to provide an interactive way to understand classical mechanics concepts through visual experimentation and real-time feedback.

Features

Adjust gravity, mass, and friction to see real-time effects on particle motion. Click anywhere on the canvas to add new particles.

Target Audience

Students, educators, and anyone interested in physics. Perfect for classroom demonstrations or self-paced learning.

Theory Behind the Simulation

Equations of Motion

The simulation uses the following equations:

aₓ = - (k / m) * vₓ
aᵧ = g - (k / m) * vᵧ

Where a is acceleration, v is velocity, k is friction coefficient, m is mass, and g is gravity.

Gravity

Gravity is modeled as a constant downward acceleration (g). On Earth, this is approximately 9.8 m/s², but you can adjust it to simulate other environments.

Friction

Friction opposes motion and is proportional to velocity. The friction coefficient (k) determines how quickly objects slow down. Set to 0 for frictionless motion.
